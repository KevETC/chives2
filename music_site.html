Music Site

The DSO Kids website is a great resource for both students and educators.

Activities section
The activities can be used by teachers to create music-based activities in the classroom. This section contains a link to music related games, as well as a make your instrument section. There is a nice variety of DIY instruments, such as xylophones, buzzing brass mouthpiece, and string instruments, to name a few. Available for download are also DSO concert coloring book, and an impressive Beethoven cut out. This can be a great resource for teachers if they feel the need to spice up their lesson plans.

Orchestra Seating Chart
The Orchestra seating chart is great for teachers to show students. This is Great for visual learners and even a great resource for new teachers.

Listen and Watch
The Listen and watch section is a great resource for students to explore. Teachers can create a worksheet based on things that students find in this part of the website.

Available for them to explore and learn about are the following.

  • Instruments - There is an extensive list of instruments with drawings, descriptions, and recordings of what they sound like.
  • Composers - This provides composers from Renaissance though Modern. Including short Biographies of all composers listed.
  • Building Blocks - Here we have basic building blocks in the key of C. there are written scales in Major and Minor(in all 3 forms) available for listen as well. We can also find the C triad, whole steps and half steps and a chart of key signatures. If that isnâ€™t enough, there is available staff paper pdf for download for students and teachers to print.

Teacher & Parent Resources
This page is separated into 4 sections.

  • Lesson Plans - Here you can find a list of lesson plans separated into categories. There is an ample amount to choose from. All lesson plans can be downloaded via PDF free of charge. My favorite is the percussion category!
  • Classroom Quick Links - Teachers can also make good use of the links provided in this page. The websites include walkSTEM experiences Dallas, Greater Dallas Youth Orchestra, Music Together Dallas, NY Phil Kids, WRR, Classical 101.1 FM, Big Thought, Classics for kids, Music Educators Toolbox, and stars & Catz. These are also resources for teachers that can be used in the classroom.
  • Bring Musicians to Your School - Here is a way to communicate with musicians and bring them to your school site. ( Only applies if you live in Dallas)
  • Games - Includes, Music memory games, flash cards, and a variety of word searches.

Conclusion
The DSO kids website is an excellent resource for both students and teachers. Students can take advantage of all of the games, visuals and listening to enhance their musical knowledge. Educators can also use those to assign to students. Teachers can also exploit the lesson plans and quick links. The website is also user friendly. I highly encourage everyone to visit this website!
